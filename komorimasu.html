Saturday, March 28

The Summit has been quiet. John and I have stayed in all week. The weather alternates between lovely sun and dreary rain. I wonder if I am deficient in vitamin D.

Time is passing in a strange way. Last weekend, I looked back at the first week and lamented how the days seemed to blend into one another, how each day felt much the same. However, looking closer, I was able to pick out reasonable differences for each day and be satisfied with that.

This week was much worse. The days melted together, my activities varied less, and my perception of elapsed time has degraded. I will look back on it as an extremely subdued and lazy spring break. Next week, classes start, and even though I'm not sure that I'm ready for structure, it will certainly help.

I am thankful for John's company, the nice space we occupy, and our ability to have delicious homecooked meals every night.

Sam and Helena baked some earl grey yogurt cake yesterday, and Adam made it today for Dhriti's birthday. I'm told they were both big hits. Happy birthday, Dhriti!

If you, a reader, also decide to join the earl grey yogurt cake cult, send me a pic and I will show it off.

In other news, today I hopped on a chartered flight and visited Helena! We ran around in the balmy sunshine, exchanged some gifts, and went clothes shopping. Helena showed me her bedroom, and I tried whacking her with a butterfly net.
